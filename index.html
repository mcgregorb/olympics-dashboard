🇺🇸 USA Athlete Spotlights
Chloe Kim • Snowboard Halfpipe🥈 Silver
Two-time Olympic champion (2018, 2022) earned silver after 17-year-old Choi Gaon's upset. Kim, 25, from Torrance, CA, fell on her final run attempting a historic three-peat while competing through a shoulder injury sustained weeks before the Games. Scored 87.75 in her best run.
Jordan Stolz • Speed Skating🥇 2x Gold
20-year-old phenom from Kewaskum, Wisconsin. Won 1000m gold with a world record and 5000m gold earlier in the Games, becoming the youngest American to win Olympic speed skating gold. Trained under renowned Dutch coach Jac Orie. Favored for 500m and 1500m events still to come.
Tess Lemley • Freestyle Moguls🥇 Gold
Led a dominant USA 1-2 finish in women's moguls alongside teammate Jaelin Kauf (silver). Delivered a technically flawless final run with superior air scores. First American woman to win Olympic moguls gold since Hannah Kearney in 2010.
Breezy Johnson • Alpine Skiing🥇 Gold
Won women's downhill gold on Day 3 in a breakthrough performance. The 29-year-old from Jackson Hole, WY, overcame two ACL surgeries during her career. DNF in Super-G on Day 6 amid fog and snow that caused 17 skiers to not finish.
Jessie Diggins • Cross-Country🥉 Bronze
From Afton, MN. Overcame bruised ribs to earn bronze in women's 10km free. Has now medaled in three consecutive Winter Olympics (2018 team sprint gold, 2022 30km silver, 2026 10km bronze) — the most decorated American cross-country skier in Olympic history with 6 career medals.
Madison Chock & Evan Bates • Ice Dance🥈 Silver
Married couple and four-time World champions known for innovative choreography. Won emotional silver in what is expected to be their final Olympic appearance, narrowly edged by France's Papadakis and Cizeron. First began skating together in 2011.
Ryan Cochran-Siegle • Alpine🥈 Silver
Vermont native earned back-to-back Olympic super-G silvers (2022 Beijing, 2026 Milano Cortina). Continues the legendary Cochran family Olympic legacy spanning five decades. Finished just behind Switzerland's Franjo von Allmen.
Alex Hall • Freestyle Slopestyle🥈 Silver
The 26-year-old from Park City, UT, earned silver in men's ski slopestyle behind Switzerland's Andri Ragettli. Known for his creative and technical style, Hall is a two-time X Games gold medalist. His silver adds to Team USA's strong freestyle showing.
